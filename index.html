Experience

Physics and Applied Mathematics Unit, ISI Kolkata
  • Developed a code to calculate N_{eff} in a model involving the SM of the universe
  • The procedure involves solving the coupled Boltzmann equations for photon and neutrino temperatures in the early Universe, incorporating non-instantaneous neutrino decoupling and finite-temperature QED corrections. The energy density evolution is governed by the Liouville equation, which is numerically integrated using Python's ODE solvers from T=10 MeV to T=10^{-2} MeV
  • N_{eff} is computed by tracking the ratio of neutrino to photon energy densities and comparing it with the standard prediction
